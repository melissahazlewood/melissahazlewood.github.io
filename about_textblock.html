About Melissa Hazlewood

Welcome and thank you for finding yourself here.

Melissa Hazlewood is a software engineer with specialties in deep learning and backend development.
She is currently building a computer vision system to detect and classify the states of plants in a balcony garden, with the ultimate goal being to connect drip irrigation and grow lights controlled using that feedback data; in other words, an automated smart garden. She is also studying system design and project management in order to collect knowledge and tools for building higher quality and more robust systems.

Previously, Melissa was part of a small software team at Bionaut Labs, a biotech startup where she primarily owned and maintained the computer vision pipeline. This required investigation into a legacy infrastructure codebase for taking raw CT scans and outputting a deep learning model for use in the Bionaut robotic surgery system. It also required injecting targeted improvements into the pipeline to increase the speed and accuracy of the model. This was done by first conducting thorough analyses of the performance of heretofor produced models, and using that information to narrow down the most promising vectors for improvement. She then led the team in implementing those improvements. For example, rebalancing the distribution of classes in the training data to represent the real-world distribution of the classes, and filtering out predictions in real time whose features contradict environmental data obtained from the surgery system sensor calibration measurements. Together, these changes led to a 77% reduction in prediction errors.

Melissa was also responsible for design, development, and testing of the automated software verification code that cut down the time it took to verify key elements of the software system from weeks to milliseconds. Besides leading a small team of engineers in finalizing the verification software, she also led the team in the final analyses of the machine learning models. This effort led to early delivery of a model that exceeded the performance of all previous models. It was also the model used in the final FDA submission, which was approved in early 2025.

She not only wrote code, but also wrote and presented technical documentation to the team and to the FDA, presented product demonstrations to investors that led to millions of dollars in funding, acted as an ad-hoc SCRUM master to keep the team motivated and on track, and so many disparate software engineering tasks that it is easier to emphasize her flexibility and work ethic than to list them all.

For a more tangible sense of Melissa and her work, please check out her projects page. There you will find a sample of her current work.
